Fleetwood Mac

An ever-changing band.

Fleetwood Mac is a band formed in London by Mick Fleetwood and John "Mac" McVie . They have been active since 1967 and have had several formations over the years. Some of its former members are Peter Green and Bob Welche. But it was with Lindsey Buckingham and Stevie Nicks as band members that they release their most commercially successful studio albums. Among these is Rumours, which topped American and British charts and has been included in multiple lists of the best albums.

Rumours

Album summary
Released on 4 February 1977. Rumours reached the top of both the US Billboard 200 and the UK Albums Chart, and became the band's most successful release.

  • 2001: ranked number 25 in Rolling Stone's special issue of "The 500 Greatest Albums of All Time".
  • 2003: inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame.
  • 2006: named in Time's "All-TIME 100 Albums" list.
Hits:
  • Don't Stop: The first single of "Rumours". This song was also used in Bill Clinton's 1994 campaign.
  • Go Your Own Way: The song expresses Buckingham's feelings in a period when Stevie Nicks and he were going through relationship issues.
  • The Chain: Only song credited to all five members in that time. Considered to have one of the most recognizable bass lines of all times.
